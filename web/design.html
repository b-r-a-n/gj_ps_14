Design Document: Flame Fighters

Made for Pirate Software's Game Jam 14

Premise

A deadly fire is spreading quickly. Players must limit the devastation by taking control of robots that can be used to extinguish the fire.

Gameplay

Overview

The player must progress through several levels of increasing difficulty. Each level represents a small area that is at risk of being overwhelmed by fire.

The player is in control of a robot in the area that has various means to prevent the fire from spreading.

Actions the robot can take represented as cards. To take an action, the player plays an available card.

Each turn the player draws a new hand of cards and the hottest parts of the fire spread.

The player does not start with a full deck of cards but gradually builds one up by choosing upgrades for their robot.

Mechanics

Level

Each level is a 2D grid of tiles. The level is complete when all burning tiles are extinguished. There may be multiple criteria for completing a level but at a minimum, the level is failed if all of the non-robot tiles are burning.

Tiles

  • Burning tile: At the end of a turn, tiles adjacent to this one have a change to start burning. This is how the fire will spread.
  • Environment tile: Part of the environment that can be interacted with. The tile may contain a material for an upgrade, a temporary powerup, an obstruction, or even something dangerous like a highly flammable material.
  • Robot tile: At the start of a level, the player's robot is placed somewhere in the environment. This is a safe tile and the starting point for the robot's fire fighting ambitions.

Turn

Progress though a level is based on turns. Each turn the player:

  • Draws until their hand is full
  • Has their energy restored
  • Plays cards from their hand until they have no more playable cards or they choose to end the turn.

At the end of the turn, tiles adjacent to burning tiles roll to determine if they start burning.

Depending on the content of the tile, there may be other effects when a tile starts to burn.
